## The Three Types of Sunlight (1:30 - 6:00)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_feiOPPacfBHHh/?t=90)**

We're designed to be outside in the sun all day, every day - not asking "what's the minimum time to get the benefit?"

### 1. Infrared Light (Red Light)

**When**: Early morning + late afternoon (as sun goes down)

**Characteristics**:
- Does NOT burn your skin
- Does NOT change skin color (no tan/sunburn)
- Does NOT push up vitamin D levels
- About **42-43% in the red light spectrum**

**Functions**:
- Incredibly healing and stimulating
- Preps your skin for UV exposure
- Heals any damage done during the day
- Red light therapy is FDA-approved for burns

### 2. Blue Light

**The key distinction:**
- Blue light is NOT inherently bad
- Blue light exists in natural sunlight
- The problem is blue light **WITHOUT red light**

**When blue light is harmful**:
- LEDs are **100% blue light** with no red
- Screens, indoor lighting = pure blue

**When blue light is good**:
- In natural sunlight, combined with abundant red light
- Morning blue light (with red) is designed to **wake you up**
- Sets brain clocks for hormone production

### 3. UV Light (Ultraviolet)

**When**: Midday (sun at highest point)

| Type | What It Does |
|------|--------------|
| **UVB** | Raises vitamin D levels, changes skin color (tan) |
| **UVA** | Raises nitric oxide levels (heart, blood vessel health, blood flow) |

## The Natural Daily Light Cycle (6:00 - 10:30)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_feiOPPacfBHHh/?t=360)**

### The Prep → Expose → Heal Cycle

| Time | Light Type | What Happens |
|------|-----------|--------------|
| **Morning** | Infrared (red) | PREPS skin to absorb UV light |
| **Midday** | UV | EXPOSES skin, builds tan, vitamin D |
| **Evening** | Infrared (red) | HEALS any damage done during day |

### Why People Get Sunburned (The Vacation Mistake)

**What most people do:**
1. Wake up late (miss morning prep)
2. Go to pool at 10am (straight into heavy UV)
3. Skin is NOT primed → BURN
4. Go inside at 3pm (miss evening healing)

**Result**: Burned, damaged skin with no healing phase.

This isn't the sun's fault - it's not knowing how to use it properly.

## Eyes and Skin Must Match (10:30 - 15:00)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_feiOPPacfBHHh/?t=630)**

It's incredibly important that your **skin and eyes** connect and understand they're experiencing the same light.

### Sunglasses: Worst Invention in History

Sunglasses **disconnect** the light going into your eyes from the light hitting your skin.

**What happens:**
- Eyes say: "It's dark"
- Skin says: "It's bright"
- Brain: "Wait... which is it?"
- **Result**: Confusion → burns → poor tanning

**Common experience**: "I don't burn anymore and I get a better tan when I don't wear sunglasses." This happens because eyes and skin are now matched.

### The Night Vision Analogy

When you're in a dark room, your eyes adjust (night vision). Your **skin does the same thing** - it goes into "night mode" and isn't ready for sun.

If you wear sunglasses:
- Your brain thinks it's dark (eyes blocked)
- Your skin is exposed to full UV
- It's like someone flipping bright lights on when your eyes are adjusted to darkness
- **Result**: Light "rips through" your skin

### Building Tolerance

If your eyes are sensitive to light:
- That means your brain is sensitive from an energy level
- You'll pay the price somewhere down the line
- You CAN build tolerance - start slow and train your eyes over time
- Don't give up and say "my eyes are too sensitive"

### What About Glasses and Contacts?

- Glass reflects and blocks certain types of sunlight
- Even prescription glasses and contacts affect light entering your eyes
- Make sure there are times you go outside with **unfettered eyes**
- Be safe (don't fall, don't drive until trained) but build tolerance

### Windows Block Light Too

Modern windows:
- Block ALL UV light
- Block **50%+ of red light**
- Let ALL blue light through

Big difference between sitting in the sun vs sitting by a window.

## Don't Use Sunscreen - Cover Instead (16:00 - 17:30)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_feiOPPacfBHHh/?t=960)**

**Question**: "What if I'm out for a really long time?"

**Answer**: Cover yourself.
- Go in the shade
- Put an umbrella up
- Wear long sleeves
- Wear a hat

### Why Not Sunscreen?

1. **Chemical sunscreens are toxic**
2. Creates **mismatch** - some sun gets through skin but eyes get full sun
3. Your body understands when skin is covered by clothing
4. Your body does NOT understand partial blocking from lotions
5. **Since sunscreens were introduced, skin cancer rates went UP**

Your body understands: "Skin is covered → no light hitting it"
Your body doesn't understand: "Skin exposed but light partially blocked by chemicals"

## Skin Color and Sun Requirements (17:30 - 21:00)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_feiOPPacfBHHh/?t=1050)**

### The Paradox Most People Find Confusing

| Skin Type | Sunlight Required |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Darker skin** | MORE sunlight needed |
| **Lighter skin** | LESS sunlight needed |

### Why This Makes Sense

**Dark skin** (high melanin):
- Evolved in high-sun environments (African plains, deserts)
- Protective mechanism against constant UV bombardment
- **Built for** very high levels of sunlight
- If you don't get high sun → you get sick

**Light skin**:
- Evolved in low-sun environments (higher latitudes)
- Skin lightened to absorb whatever UV was available
- Need less sun to get the same benefit

### The Fitzgerald Score

A scale that tells you what color your skin is.
- **Higher score** = darker skin = more sunlight needed
- Use this to calculate how much time you need outside

### Location Matters

- Longitude and latitude make a huge difference
- Closer to equator = more consistent sunlight = typically better health
- If you can move closer to equator, that's the preferred option
- Stronger sun = less time needed outside
- Weaker sun = more time needed outside

## More Daytime Light = Better Nighttime Sleep (22:00 - 23:30)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_feiOPPacfBHHh/?t=1320)**

When people have trouble sleeping, ask about their **daytime light exposure**.

"What does that have to do with it?"

If your body doesn't get enough light during the day, it won't know to **shut off at night**. You have to fill each "canister" to move to the next step.

### The Beach Phenomenon

Ever notice people fall asleep on the beach?

- That level of UV light is designed to calm your system
- Shut the engines down
- Decrease hormone production
- Decrease cortisol

**If you get tired from midday sun exposure - that's a GOOD sign.** Your body is doing what it's supposed to.

(Early morning sun should wake you up. Midday sun calms you down.)

## Summary

| Light Type | Time | Function | Notes |
|-----------|------|----------|-------|
| **Infrared (Red)** | Morning | Wake up, prep skin | Sets hormones, no skin color change |
| **UV (UVA/UVB)** | Midday | Vitamin D, nitric oxide, tan | Need morning prep first |
| **Infrared (Red)** | Evening | Heal skin damage | Sets body for sleep |

**Key Takeaways:**
- Blue light is only toxic when not accompanied by red light
- Sunglasses disconnect eyes from skin → burns and poor tanning
- The Prep → Expose → Heal cycle prevents sunburn
- Don't use sunscreen - cover up instead
- Darker skin = more sunlight needed; lighter skin = less needed
- Use D Minder app to calculate your sun time
- More daytime light = better nighttime sleep
- Train your eyes gradually to tolerate sun without glasses
- Windows block UV and 50%+ red light but let all blue through
