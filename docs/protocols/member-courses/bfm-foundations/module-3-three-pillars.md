## Pillar 1: Redux Potential (1:00 - 10:30)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_TSkGMiT8VDE7P/?t=60)**

### Basic Science Review

You may remember from elementary school there are three types of particles:
- **Electrons** - negatively charged
- **Protons** - positively charged
- **Neutrons** - no charge

### Electrons as Currency

Inside the human body, **electrons act as currency**. In my energetic debt model, electrons are the energy your body uses to form ATP - what your body uses like gasoline for a car.

Inside your mitochondria is an **electron transport chain**. All the food you eat (protein, carbohydrates, fat) - the purpose of the body is to strip the electrons off of them to put them in this engine of the mitochondria.

**There is no protein transport chain, no carbohydrate transport chain, no fat transport chain - only an electron transport chain.**

### The Balance of Charges

- **More electrons** (negative charge) = like having money in a savings account
- **More protons than electrons** (positive charge) = **inflammation**

We want more **negative charge** in the human body. It's weird to think that, but we want more negativity - not in emotion, but in electrical charge.

### What is Redux?

Redux combines two words:
- **Reduction** = gaining an electron (reduces the charge)
- **Oxidation** = losing an electron

**Oxidation means inflammation.** Think of iron that oxidizes - it rusts. Inside us, oxidation causes rust, damage, inflammatory processes.

### Redux Potential = Bail Money

Think of redux potential as **bail money**. If you get in trouble, can you bail yourself out of it? It's nice to know that if something goes wrong, it's there to help you.

**Someone with high redux potential can reduce oxidative stress and inflammation from different traumas.** That's what makes some people "bulletproof" - they can traumatize themselves with bad choices and still reduce the inflammation because they carry very high redux potential.

### The Two Components of Redux Potential

**1. Chemical Component**
- **Antioxidants** are substances that donate electrons
- They have a free electron they're happy to give
- Like giving somebody cash to pay a bill
- Important but not the whole picture

**2. Physics/Energetic Component** (often overlooked)
- The mitochondria itself must carry a very high electrical charge
- There is literally a **30 million volt charge** on the inside of your mitochondrial membranes
- That's the equivalent of lightning
- This charge cannot be put in a pill or bottle
- **You have to earn it through your connection with the environment**

### Why You Can't Just Take Antioxidants

Everyone agrees chronic inflammation is bad. But it's not as simple as "just take antioxidants."

**We need some oxidation.** The body:
- Signals through oxidation
- Repairs itself through oxidation
- Replaces cells through oxidation

This is why oncologists sometimes conflict with natural practitioners - conventional cancer therapy **needs** oxidation to kill cancer cells. Taking tons of antioxidants can make cancer cells resistant to treatment.

### The Fire and Smoke Analogy

**Inflammation is smoke coming off a fire** - it doesn't cause the fire, it's just the smoke.

If you just break out windows and push all the smoke out, you don't put out the fire. The fire will eventually take down the structure.

Taking antioxidants will help knock the smoke down, **but it won't put out the fire**.

## Pillar 2: Heteroplasmy (10:30 - 20:00)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_TSkGMiT8VDE7P/?t=630)**

### Two Types of DNA

**Nuclear DNA:**
- 50% from mom, 50% from dad
- Determines your structure (how tall, hair color, eye color)
- Determines **what disease you will get** if you get one
- Heavily protected (one set per cell)
- Like your computer's **hard drive** - long-term storage

**Mitochondrial DNA:**
- **100% from your mother** (dads play no role)
- Not discovered until about 40 years ago
- Much more abundant (thousands per cell)
- Less protected because it must sense the environment
- Determines **IF you will get a disease at all**
- Like your computer's **RAM** - speed and efficiency

### What is Heteroplasmy?

**Heteroplasmy = damage to mitochondrial DNA**

The percent of heteroplasmy means the amount of mitochondrial DNA damage in the body.

### The Key Relationship

> **Nuclear DNA loads the gun, but mitochondrial DNA pulls the trigger.**

If mitochondrial DNA is not damaged, nuclear DNA never has to express disease because there's no reason to.

### The 10% Rule

You get a **10% heteroplasmic increase every decade**. This is normal - no one stays here forever.

- After about **70% damage**, you start getting symptoms and disease diagnoses
- Life expectancy (late 70s) correlates with 70-80% heteroplasmic rate
- People who live into their 80s, 90s, or 100s have **lower heteroplasmic rates**

Lower heteroplasmic rates usually relate to good redux potential.

### Over 300 Diseases Mapped

Researchers have mapped over **300 different chronic diseases** to specific mitochondrial DNA damage:

| Damage Location | Damage % | Disease |
|-----------------|----------|---------|
| Part A | 30% | Diabetes |
| Part B | 50% | Retinitis pigmentosa |
| Part C | 70% | Alzheimer's |
| Part D | 70% | Parkinson's |

You're not hearing this in media because **there are no pills that can work on this** - none in production, none in the research pipeline.

### How the Immune System Responds

When mitochondrial DNA takes damage, it signals to the nuclear DNA using **inflammation as a flare**:

"Hey, we need to shoot up a flare so we can let the immune system know that we need help."

Your immune system comes in to help repair damage - **it's not attacking by mistake**. It's responding to a flare saying "we're hurt, it's your job to come help us."

### The Danger of Suppressing Inflammation

When we take over-the-counter anti-inflammatories (Tylenol, aspirin) or immune suppressors for autoimmune disease, **all we're doing is stopping the body from responding to the help signal**.

The damage is still happening. We're just knocking down someone's ability to call 911 and ask for help.

## Pillar 3: Autophagy and Apoptosis (20:00 - 25:00)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_TSkGMiT8VDE7P/?t=1200)**

There are two branches of immune function:

### Autophagy = Repair

**Autophagy** is your body's ability through your immune system to **repair damage** to cellular structures.

In the insurance adjuster analogy: "This isn't too bad. It pays to spend the money and energy to fix this."

**If autophagy gets damaged, someone will get an autoimmune disease.**

### Apoptosis = Replace

**Apoptosis** is when the insurance adjuster says: "This car is totaled. Don't spend any money on it. Just get rid of it and get a new one."

**If apoptosis gets damaged:**
- The person will get **cancer** or
- A **neurodegenerative disease**

### What Happens When Apoptosis Fails

Cells start running through too quickly. You're left with cells that are essentially old - they can't function, they're not supposed to be there, they should be retired, but they're still in the workforce.

These cells say: "I guess I'm just going to have to do whatever I can to survive." They start **breaking all the rules** and rapidly dividing - which is what cancer is.

When this happens in the brain and you go through cells too fast, your brain can't replace them quickly enough. The brain starts to atrophy and shrink - that's **neurodegenerative disorders**.
