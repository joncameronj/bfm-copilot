## The Complete Morning Protocol (1:30 - 3:00)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_DUNRvQTeZXHHS/?t=90)**

### Be Outside, Grounded, at Sunrise

**Grounding** = being in contact with the earth (barefoot)

| Surface | Conductivity |
|---------|--------------|
| **Grass** | Best |
| **Dirt** | Good |
| **Concrete** | Less conduction |

**Caveat**: Don't ground above buried power lines. There are ways to test for this.

### Why Hands and Feet?

Humans are the **only mammals with sweat glands on their hands and feet**. This is why grounding through hands and feet works so well.

### The Complete Circuit

When you:
- Ground (connect to earth to get electrons)
- Get sunlight directly into your eyes
- Have maximum skin exposure

You create a **complete electrical circuit**. The sun charges the energy your body is getting from the ground and helps reset all your rhythms completely.

### What to Avoid

- No contacts
- No glasses
- No makeup
- No lotions on skin
- As much skin exposure as possible

### Hybrid Tanning: Building Up

Early morning light **preps your body** to absorb more UV light later. This is called "hybrid tanning."

You may need to:
- Start very slow
- Build up over time
- Adjust for your skin type
- Consider your current health status

## Testing Your Light Exposure (3:30 - 6:00)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_DUNRvQTeZXHHS/?t=210)**

Two ways to measure if you're getting enough light:

### 1. Vitamin D Levels

**What it measures**: UVB light exposure (middle-of-day sun that tans your skin)

Vitamin D is a **proxy** for how much light you're getting.

**The reality:**
- Almost everyone we check is catastrophically low
- The sicker someone is, the lower their vitamin D

**Why supplements don't count:**
- Taking vitamin D supplements skews bloodwork
- But you don't get the same outcomes as sun exposure
- Research shows: more sunlight = longer lifespan, less all-cause mortality (including skin cancers!)
- These benefits **only apply to sun-derived vitamin D**, not supplements

> **Vitamin D from supplements is not one-to-one with vitamin D from the sun. It will never be the same thing.**

### 2. Nitric Oxide Test Strips

**What it measures**: Infrared and UVA light (earlier morning light)

- Done through saliva
- Can do at home without a doctor
- Shows if levels are high, low, or non-existent

### When to Use Them

- When you can't get outside
- Scheduling doesn't allow morning sun
- Your location has limited sunlight
- As a helpful adjunct (not replacement) for natural sun

**Nothing replaces the full spectrum of sunlight.**

### What to Look For

| Feature | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| **Right frequencies** | Different wavelengths do different things |
| **No flicker** | LED flicker stresses nervous system (imperceptible but harmful) |
| **Low EMF** | Powerful devices often emit electromagnetic fields |

### The Flicker Problem

LED lights flicker at a rate imperceptible to the human eye. If you video one in slow-mo, you'd see it.

This flicker:
- Is incredibly annoying to your nervous system
- Can trigger seizures in susceptible people (like strobing in movies)
- Negates some of the benefit of the light therapy

### CRITICAL: Timing Matters

**Do NOT use red light therapy at night!**

Many people excitedly tell me: "Every night before bed, I run the light therapy device!"

Your body interprets this as morning light. Then you want to sleep for 8 hours. **Confusion leads to problems.**

Red light therapy devices = **first thing in the morning only**.

## Indoor Lighting Solutions (10:30 - 13:00)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_DUNRvQTeZXHHS/?t=630)**

### Full Spectrum Light Bulbs

Since LED/blue lighting is terrible for you, consider full spectrum bulbs that:
- Come as close to sunlight as possible
- Have **different settings** for morning, midday, and nighttime (no blue, more red)
- Allow your body to adapt to proper light cycles indoors

### The Classroom Study

They compared traditional blue lighting vs full spectrum lighting in classrooms:

| Traditional Blue Lighting | Full Spectrum Lighting |
|--------------------------|------------------------|
| Normal grades | **Grades went up** |
| ADHD present | **ADHD basically didn't exist** |
| Normal dental health | **No cavities** |

This shows the power of proper light vs alien light.

### The Light Break Rule

For every **hour in blue light**, try to spend at least **5 minutes in the sun** to offset the damage.

Modern schools with constant screens and little recess/outdoor time are especially problematic.

### Evening Light Rules

- Indoor lighting needs to **come down at night**
- Can't use same strength light as morning
- Amber bulbs for night use
- Light keeps the body revving and prevents entering sleep cycles
