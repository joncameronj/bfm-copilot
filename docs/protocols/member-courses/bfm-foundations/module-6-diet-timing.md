## The Food-Light Mismatch Problem (7:00 - 9:30)

### The Banana in Alaska Problem

You're eating a "healthy" banana. But:
- You live in Alaska (24 hours of darkness)
- The banana grew in Brazilian jungles (very high light)
- **Result**: Healthy food becomes a mismatch → confusion → inflammation

**When you eat food grown in high sunlight, your body assumes YOU are in high sunlight.**

If those don't match, you inflame - even from "healthy" foods eaten out of season.

### Solution: Eat Local and Seasonal

- Talk to local farmers
- Shop at farmers markets
- Google what grows in your area
- Eat foods that would naturally be available where you live

## Meal Timing Strategy (9:30 - 12:00)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_bZ2t4HbB92Gg5/?t=570)**

### Stop Grazing

"Eat small meals throughout the day" is literally the **worst thing you can do**. It's not how the body is designed.

You're designed to eat bursts of meals with big spacing gaps between.

### The Two-Meal Target

| Meal | When | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **Meal 1** | Within 30 minutes of waking | Protein/fat heavy |
| **Meal 2** | After UV light drops | Use D Minder app to check |
| **Last meal** | 3-4 hours before bed | Body needs time to digest before healing |

### Why UV Timing Matters

In the middle of the day when UV light is high, it **shuts down the parts of your mitochondria where food energy enters**. Your body is getting energy from light - it doesn't need food then.

Eat around **infrared light spectrums**, not UV spectrums.

### Late Night Eating Problem

Even if your light cycles are perfect, eating late confuses your body:

"I would love to start healing, but I can't heal until I'm done with this job - digesting this food."

Healing gets postponed until digestion is complete, eating into your 8-hour healing cycle.
