## The 4-Hour Rule Revisited (3:00 - 5:30)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_KvvyZ3Vobr6ve/?t=180)**

### The Math Problem

Melatonin needs **4 hours of darkness** before your body kicks into healing/repair mode.

**Most people's pattern:**
- Last light = right before bed (bathroom, phone, alarm)
- Average sleep = 6 hours
- 4 hours for darkness clock + 6 hours sleep = **only 2 hours of healing**

That's **25%** of what you need. You will not get better or stay well on 25% healing.

### What Should Happen

The sun goes down → infrared light winds you down → darkness triggers the 4-hour clock → by bedtime, you enter healing mode immediately → 8 hours of regenerative sleep.

### What Actually Happens

Sun goes down → lights flip on → constant light exposure → look at phone before bed (tells brain it's noon) → body confused about why we're sleeping at "noon" → 4-hour clock starts at bedtime → only 2 hours healing.

## Blue Blocker Glasses (6:30 - 8:00)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_KvvyZ3Vobr6ve/?t=390)**

### When to Wear Them

- **DO NOT wear during suntime** - when the sun is up
- **Wear after sun goes down**
- **Keep on until complete darkness** in your bedroom
- Take off only when all lights are off and you're going to bed

### How They Work

They block the blue spectrum of light that signals "daytime" to your brain. This tricks your system into thinking there's no light available.

### The 9-Second Rule

**It takes about 9 seconds of eye exposure to light before your brain kicks on and says "it must be daytime."**

We're programmed for this because man-made light never existed in our evolution. We just don't understand it.

### Quality Matters

Many blue blockers are junk - they don't actually block all spectrums. Get ones that have been tested with spectrophotometers.

## Cover Your Skin (8:00 - 9:30)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_KvvyZ3Vobr6ve/?t=480)**

Just like during the day (eyes and skin need to match), at night we don't want:
- Eyes blocked from light
- Skin getting light

### Simple Solutions

- **Wear long sleeves** (not short sleeves)
- **Wear pants** (not shorts)
- **Cover your throat area** - especially if:
  - You have thyroid problems
  - You're looking at phones/screens (throat faces the light)

This creates cohesion between what your eyes sense and what your skin senses.

### Screen Solutions

- Blue blocking screens for computers
- Keep screen brightness down
- Minimize screen time at night

## Your Bedroom: A Sleep Sanctuary (9:30 - 12:00)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_KvvyZ3Vobr6ve/?t=570)**

### Pitch Black

Once you're asleep, your room must be **pitch black** - as dark as it can be.

### Common Light Sources to Eliminate

| Light Source | Solution |
|--------------|----------|
| Digital alarm clock | Battery-powered old school analog clock |
| Street lights through windows | Blackout curtains |
| Smoke alarm light | Put tape over it |
| TV standby lights | Unplug or cover |
| Phone/tablet screens | Remove from bedroom |

### The Hashimoto's Story

A patient got her Hashimoto's completely resolved. Years later, it came back. What changed? She got divorced, lived alone for the first time in 20+ years, and started sleeping with the TV on again.

The second she stopped watching TV at night, Hashimoto's went right back away.

**Your body is THIS tuned to light.**

### Bedroom Rules

- Your bedroom is for **sleeping**
- Not for watching TV
- Not for playing video games
- No electronics
- No TVs at night

### Middle of the Night Bathroom Trips

If you get up and turn lights on - after 9 seconds, your body says "okay, it's daytime" and you restart the 4-hour clock all over again.

Solution: Navigate in the dark or use amber/red night lights only.

## Two Critical Sleep Environment Factors (13:30 - 15:30)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_KvvyZ3Vobr6ve/?t=810)**

### 1. EMF-Free Environment

Your sleeping area needs to be **completely EMF free**:
- Nothing plugged in near you
- No Wi-Fi
- No cell phones near you

EMF will screw up your melatonin action. (More in the EMF module.)

### 2. Food Timing

One of the biggest things: **don't eat before bedtime**.

If you eat late:
- Body can't shut engines down to heal
- Still has the job of digesting food
- Insulin levels can't drop

**Even if your light timing is perfect**, your body will say: "That's great, but I can't go into healing mode yet because I still have this work to do from digesting food."

Late-night dessert is especially problematic - keeps insulin levels up.
