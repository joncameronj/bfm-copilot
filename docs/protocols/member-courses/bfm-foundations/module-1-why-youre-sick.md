## The Body's Core Function: Energy Production (1:30 - 2:30)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_Owh6kRoncIROl/?t=90)**

If you break down all the different things we do throughout the course of a day, the entire body is simply about one thing: **we need to constantly make energy**.

Energy is our most prized possession. If you took anybody in the world, whether incredibly sick or incredibly healthy, and asked "Do you want more energy?" - the answer is invariably yes. Even the most energetic person would say "Absolutely, I want more energy."

The human body is built to make energy. That is its core function. If it was a business, its core product would be: "We need to constantly make enough energy to run this very complex structure."

## Traditional Medicine: The Mendelian Model (2:30 - 4:00)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_Owh6kRoncIROl/?t=150)**

Years ago, there was a guy named Mendel who discovered DNA. Then we got the Mendelian genetic model of disease which basically says: you go to the doctor and they tell you "you have high cholesterol because your parents had high cholesterol" or "you got this disease because it's in your genetic line."

The Mendelian ideology of disease says: **if you have a problem like a headache, there is obviously some sort of problem with your head**. Maybe you've experienced this - you have a problem with your head, you get MRIs or imaging, and your head looks fine. So it can't be coming from that, right?

## The Bioenergetic Model: A Different Approach (4:00 - 5:30)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_Owh6kRoncIROl/?t=240)**

We want to introduce you to a different ideology called the **bioenergetic model of disease**. This basically states that your body makes a tremendous amount of energy, and each part uses certain amounts of it.

Your brain uses about **25% of all the energy your body makes**. It's the energy hog - it gets first dibs. Think of your brain as the CEO taking 25% of the profits.

### The CEO Analogy

If somewhere else in the business (that has nothing to do with the CEO) the profit margins drop, that 25% profit the CEO takes is going to drop as well. If his normal salary is a million dollars a year because he's taking 25% of profits, but the business drops by 25%, he's going to get 25% less money.

That's going to cause symptoms in that part of the body. But when you look at an image or look directly at that area with narrow vision saying "the problem must be right there," you're going to miss the fact that maybe the problem is in the liver. The liver is not producing as much energy as it should, and now the CEO's profits get affected - but it won't show a problem directly with the CEO.

This answers why so many people say: "Doctor, my blood work looks fine. I did imaging and everything looks fine." Great - that means there's nothing structurally wrong with that area. But **80% of chronic disease problems don't come from genetic or structural issues**.

## Symptoms as Adaptation, Not Mistakes (5:30 - 7:30)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_Owh6kRoncIROl/?t=330)**

You have to switch your thinking. When somebody gets symptoms, we immediately assume the body has made a giant mistake - it's acting out of character and done something wrong.

The reality is: **the body hasn't done something wrong. It's adapting to overcome some sort of problem.**

It's not "what's wrong?" The question really needs to be: **"Why would it do that?"**

This is incredibly powerful because it changes linear thinking. Instead of saying "your body is dumb, it's made this mistake, we're going to give you pills to cover up the mistake" - we use critical thinking: "Wait, why would the body do that?"

The body knows what it needs to do. If you've ever looked at the complexity of the body and how many things it does second-to-second, it's incredibly complicated. When it starts making decisions and things go awry, it's not doing it because it's wrong or stupid. **It's doing it because it has to overcome some issue it's run into.**

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_Owh6kRoncIROl/?t=450)**

While the medical world is obsessed with diagnosing symptoms or naming diseases, no one's really asking: **What's the energetic status of the body?**

When the body goes into low energy, it will absolutely go into survival mode. It will **steal from Peter to pay Paul** because there is a specific pecking order.

If it comes down to making gender hormones (estrogens, progesterones, testosterones) versus running your brain - it's not a fair fight. The brain gets first dibs. Always. There is no other chance.

We need to ask **why** it's happening, not assume the body is broken or stupid.

## Mitochondria: The Real Control Center (8:30 - 11:00)

**[Watch this section](https://whop.com/joined/beyond-functional-medicine/exp_ZLhOFH8OcvlPRV/app/courses/cors_LstpN0QzwLAaf/lessons/lesn_Owh6kRoncIROl/?t=510)**

The bioenergetic model is based on **mitochondria**. You may remember from elementary school: "the mitochondria, the powerhouse of the cell." They're the little engines inside your cells whose job is to make energy.

But here's what most people don't know: **mitochondria have their own DNA**.

### Nuclear DNA vs Mitochondrial DNA

- **Nuclear DNA**: In the nucleus of your cells. 50% from mom, 50% from dad. Determines what you look like, your structure.
- **Mitochondrial DNA**: Discovered only about 20-40 years ago. Significantly more complicated than nuclear DNA. There's a lot more of it. **This is what determines what your body does and when it does it.**

Your mitochondrial DNA is really driving the bus - telling the body what to do. Then those same mitochondria make energy to provide all these functions. Your nuclear DNA says "here's what we're supposed to be doing" but it's all run by mitochondrial DNA.

When we end up in energy loss, this is not random. The body goes into **biological triage** - like going into the emergency room where they have steps: first we do this, then we do that. When the body breaks down, it triages and says "this is what we have to do first, this is who gets priority."

**That's all run by the mitochondrial DNA.**
