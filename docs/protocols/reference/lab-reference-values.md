# Lab Reference Values Quick Guide

## Overview

This guide provides quick reference for optimal vs conventional lab ranges. Use these values to identify suboptimal levels that may not be flagged on standard lab reports.

## Thyroid Panel

| Marker | Conventional | Optimal | Unit |
|--------|--------------|---------|------|
| TSH | 0.45-4.5 | 1.0-2.0 | mIU/L |
| Free T4 | 0.82-1.77 | 1.0-1.5 | ng/dL |
| Free T3 | 2.0-4.4 | 3.0-4.0 | pg/mL |
| Reverse T3 | 9.2-24.1 | 10-15 | ng/dL |
| TPO Antibodies | < 34 | < 10 | IU/mL |
| Thyroglobulin Ab | < 0.9 | < 0.5 | IU/mL |

## Notes

- **Optimal ranges** are targets for functional health, not just absence of disease
- Individual factors (age, gender, symptoms) may shift optimal targets
- Trends over time are often more informative than single values
- Always interpret labs in context of symptoms and clinical picture
