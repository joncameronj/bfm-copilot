# Chronic Inflammation Care Protocol

## Overview

Chronic inflammation underlies many modern health conditions including autoimmune diseases, cardiovascular disease, metabolic syndrome, and cognitive decline. This protocol provides a comprehensive approach to identifying and addressing inflammatory drivers.

## Understanding Inflammation

### Acute vs Chronic Inflammation

**Acute Inflammation** (Beneficial)
- Short-term immune response
- Responds to injury or infection
- Resolves naturally

**Chronic Inflammation** (Harmful)
- Persistent, low-grade inflammation
- Often "silent" with no obvious symptoms
- Drives tissue damage and disease

### Common Symptoms of Chronic Inflammation

- Fatigue
- Body aches and joint pain
- Brain fog
- Skin issues (eczema, acne)
- Digestive problems
- Weight gain/difficulty losing weight
- Depression/anxiety
- Frequent infections

## Evaluation

### Inflammatory Markers

| Marker | Optimal | High Risk | Notes |
|--------|---------|-----------|-------|
| hs-CRP | < 1.0 mg/L | > 3.0 mg/L | Most sensitive general marker |
| ESR | < 10 mm/hr | > 20 mm/hr | Non-specific, chronic conditions |
| Homocysteine | 6-8 umol/L | > 12 umol/L | CV risk, methylation |
| Fibrinogen | 200-300 mg/dL | > 400 mg/dL | Clotting, CV risk |
| Ferritin | 50-100 ng/mL | > 200 ng/mL | High = inflammation |

### Additional Testing to Consider

- **Fasting insulin** - Insulin resistance drives inflammation
- **HbA1c** - Blood sugar dysregulation
- **Complete thyroid panel** - Autoimmune thyroid
- **Food sensitivity panel** - IgG antibodies
- **Gut testing** - Stool analysis for dysbiosis
- **Omega-3 index** - Fatty acid balance

## Root Causes of Chronic Inflammation

### 1. Gut Dysfunction
- Leaky gut (intestinal permeability)
- Dysbiosis (bacterial imbalance)
- SIBO
- Food sensitivities
- H. pylori, candida

### 2. Dietary Factors
- Processed foods
- Refined sugars
- Industrial seed oils (canola, soybean, corn)
- Gluten (in sensitive individuals)
- Excessive alcohol

### 3. Environmental Toxins
- Mold exposure
- Heavy metals
- Pesticides/herbicides
- Air pollution

### 4. Chronic Infections
- Epstein-Barr virus (EBV)
- Lyme disease
- Dental infections
- Sinus infections

### 5. Lifestyle Factors
- Chronic stress
- Poor sleep
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Overtraining
- Obesity

## Treatment Protocol

### Step 1: Remove Inflammatory Triggers

**Dietary Elimination (30-60 days)**
- Gluten
- Dairy
- Refined sugar
- Processed foods
- Industrial seed oils
- Alcohol

**Environmental**
- Assess home for mold
- Filter drinking water
- Use clean personal care products
- Consider air purifier

### Step 2: Anti-Inflammatory Diet

**Foods to Emphasize**
- Wild-caught fatty fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel)
- Colorful vegetables (especially leafy greens)
- Berries (blueberries, blackberries)
- Healthy fats (olive oil, avocado, coconut)
- Nuts and seeds (walnuts, flax, chia)
- Herbs and spices (turmeric, ginger, rosemary)
- Bone broth

**Cooking Methods**
- Avoid high-heat cooking with oils
- Prefer steaming, baking, slow-cooking
- Minimize charred/blackened foods

### Step 3: Targeted Supplementation

**Core Anti-Inflammatory Stack**

| Supplement | Dose | Timing | Notes |
|------------|------|--------|-------|
| Omega-3 (EPA/DHA) | 2-4 g/day | With meals | Target EPA:DHA ratio 2:1 |
| Curcumin | 500-1000 mg | Twice daily | With fat and black pepper |
| Vitamin D3 | 2000-5000 IU | Morning | Immune modulation |
| Magnesium | 400-600 mg | Evening | Glycinate or malate form |
| SPM (Pro-resolving mediators) | As directed | Daily | Resolves inflammation |

**Additional Options**

| Supplement | Indication | Dose |
|------------|------------|------|
| Quercetin | Allergies, mast cell | 500 mg twice daily |
| Resveratrol | Cardiovascular, aging | 200-500 mg daily |
| NAC | Glutathione support | 600-1200 mg daily |
| Boswellia | Joint inflammation | 300-500 mg twice daily |
| Ginger | Digestive inflammation | 500 mg twice daily |

### Step 4: Address Gut Health

1. **Remove pathogens** - Treat infections, overgrowths
2. **Replace** - Digestive enzymes, HCl if needed
3. **Reinoculate** - Probiotics, fermented foods
4. **Repair** - L-glutamine, collagen, zinc

See: Leaky Gut Protocol for detailed gut healing protocol

### Step 5: Lifestyle Modifications

**Stress Management**
- Daily meditation or breathwork (10+ min)
- Regular nature exposure
- Social connection
- Limit news/social media

**Sleep Optimization**
- 7-9 hours nightly
- Consistent sleep/wake times
- Dark, cool room
- No screens 1 hour before bed

**Movement**
- Regular moderate exercise
- Avoid excessive cardio
- Include strength training
- Walking in nature

- Energy levels

- Skin appearance

- Weight changes

## Related Conditions

Chronic inflammation is linked to:
- **Autoimmune diseases** - Hashimoto's, RA, Lupus
- **Metabolic syndrome** - Insulin resistance, obesity
- **Cardiovascular disease** - Atherosclerosis
- **Neurological** - Depression, Alzheimer's
- **Gut conditions** - IBD, IBS
