# Adrenal Fatigue Care Protocol

## Overview

Adrenal fatigue, or HPA (Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal) axis dysfunction, occurs when the body's stress response system becomes dysregulated. This protocol addresses evaluation and management of adrenal dysfunction.

## Understanding Adrenal Dysfunction

### Stages of Adrenal Dysfunction

1. **Stage 1: Alarm (High Cortisol)**
   - Body in constant fight-or-flight
   - Elevated cortisol throughout day
   - Symptoms: Anxiety, insomnia, weight gain (especially abdominal)

2. **Stage 2: Resistance (Dysregulated)**
   - Cortisol pattern becomes erratic
   - Morning cortisol may be low, spikes at wrong times
   - Symptoms: Fatigue with bursts of energy, wired-but-tired

3. **Stage 3: Exhaustion (Low Cortisol)**
   - Cortisol production significantly reduced
   - Flat cortisol curve throughout day
   - Symptoms: Severe fatigue, difficulty handling stress, low blood pressure

## Initial Evaluation

### Required Testing

1. **4-Point Salivary Cortisol** (preferred)
   - Morning (upon waking)
   - Noon
   - Afternoon
   - Night (before bed)

2. **DHEA-S** (blood test)
   - Evaluate adrenal reserve
   - Cortisol/DHEA ratio

3. **Related Labs**
   - Complete thyroid panel (thyroid-adrenal axis)
   - Fasting glucose/insulin (cortisol affects blood sugar)
   - Sodium/potassium (aldosterone function)

### Optimal Cortisol Ranges (Salivary)

| Time | Optimal Range | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------|
| Morning (7-8am) | 10-20 ng/mL | Should be highest |
| Noon | 5-10 ng/mL | Gradual decline |
| Afternoon (4pm) | 3-6 ng/mL | Continued decline |
| Night (10-11pm) | 1-3 ng/mL | Should be lowest |

### Cortisol Awakening Response (CAR)

- Cortisol should rise 50-100% within 30 min of waking
- Blunted CAR indicates HPA dysfunction
- Measure: upon waking + 30 min after waking

## Treatment Protocols

### Stage 1: High Cortisol Protocol

**Goals**: Calm the stress response, lower cortisol

1. **Adaptogens** (calming)
   - Ashwagandha: 300-600 mg twice daily
   - Holy Basil: 500 mg twice daily
   - Phosphatidylserine: 100-300 mg at night

2. **Lifestyle**
   - Strict sleep hygiene (bed by 10pm)
   - Reduce caffeine (none after noon)
   - Daily meditation/breathwork
   - Moderate exercise only (no HIIT)

3. **Supplements**
   - Magnesium glycinate: 400-600 mg at night
   - L-theanine: 200 mg as needed for anxiety
   - GABA: 500-750 mg at night if needed

### Stage 2: Dysregulated Cortisol Protocol

**Goals**: Restore normal cortisol rhythm

1. **Adaptogens** (balancing)
   - Rhodiola: 200-400 mg in morning
   - Ashwagandha: 300-600 mg at night
   - Licorice root: 200-400 mg in morning (if low morning cortisol)

2. **Lifestyle**
   - Consistent wake time (same every day)
   - Morning light exposure within 30 min of waking
   - Eating within 1 hour of waking
   - No screens after 8pm

3. **Supplements**
   - B-complex (methylated): Morning
   - Vitamin C: 1000-2000 mg divided doses
   - Pantothenic acid (B5): 500 mg

### Stage 3: Low Cortisol Protocol

**Goals**: Support adrenal recovery, gently increase cortisol

1. **Adrenal Support** (stimulating)
   - Adrenal glandulars: As directed
   - Licorice root: 400-800 mg morning/noon (monitor BP)
   - Rhodiola: 200-400 mg morning
   - Vitamin C: 2000-3000 mg divided

2. **Lifestyle**
   - Extended rest periods
   - No intense exercise (walking, yoga only)
   - Increase salt intake (sea salt)
   - Frequent small meals (don't skip!)

3. **Critical Considerations**
   - Rule out Addison's disease if cortisol very low
   - May need temporary hydrocortisone support
   - Recovery takes 6-24 months

## Thyroid-Adrenal Connection

**Important**: Do not aggressively treat thyroid without addressing adrenals first.

- Low cortisol impairs T4 to T3 conversion
- Thyroid medication with low cortisol can worsen symptoms
- Address adrenals first, then optimize thyroid

## Diet Recommendations

### Foods to Include
- Quality proteins at every meal
- Healthy fats (avocado, olive oil, coconut)
- Complex carbs (sweet potato, squash)
- Sea salt liberally
- Bone broth

### Foods to Avoid
- Caffeine (especially in stage 3)
- Sugar and refined carbs
- Alcohol
- Processed foods

## Follow-Up Schedule

- **Initial**: Retest 4-point cortisol at 8-12 weeks
- **Ongoing**: Every 3-4 months until stable
- **Maintenance**: Annual assessment

## Related Conditions

Always evaluate for:
- **Thyroid dysfunction** - Adrenal-thyroid axis
- **Blood sugar dysregulation** - Cortisol affects glucose
- **Sleep disorders** - Cortisol rhythm affects sleep
- **Chronic infections** - EBV, Lyme stress adrenals
