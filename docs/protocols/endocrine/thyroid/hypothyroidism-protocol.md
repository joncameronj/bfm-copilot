# Hypothyroidism Care Protocol

## Overview

Hypothyroidism is a condition where the thyroid gland does not produce enough thyroid hormones. This protocol outlines the BFM approach to evaluation, diagnosis, and management.

## Initial Evaluation

### Required Labs

1. **Full Thyroid Panel**
   - TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone)
   - Free T4 (Thyroxine)
   - Free T3 (Triiodothyronine)
   - Reverse T3 (when indicated)
   - TPO Antibodies (Thyroid Peroxidase)
   - Thyroglobulin Antibodies

2. **Related Labs to Consider**
   - Ferritin (iron stores affect T4 to T3 conversion)
   - Vitamin D (immune modulation)
   - Cortisol (adrenal-thyroid axis)
   - Selenium (thyroid hormone metabolism)
   - Iodine (if deficiency suspected)

### Optimal vs Conventional Ranges

| Marker | Conventional Range | Optimal Range |
|--------|-------------------|---------------|
| TSH | 0.45-4.5 mIU/L | 1.0-2.0 mIU/L |
| Free T4 | 0.82-1.77 ng/dL | 1.0-1.5 ng/dL |
| Free T3 | 2.0-4.4 pg/mL | 3.0-4.0 pg/mL |
| Reverse T3 | 9.2-24.1 ng/dL | < 15 ng/dL |
| TPO Ab | < 34 IU/mL | < 10 IU/mL |

## Ominous Markers

The following patterns require immediate attention:

1. **TSH > 10 mIU/L** - Indicates significant hypothyroidism
2. **TPO Antibodies > 500 IU/mL** - Active autoimmune attack
3. **Free T3 < 2.0 pg/mL** - Severe T3 deficiency
4. **Reverse T3 > 20 ng/dL with Low Free T3** - Conversion problem

## Treatment Approach

### Step 1: Address Root Causes

Before or alongside medication:
- **Check iron status** - Ferritin should be 70-90 ng/mL for optimal thyroid function
- **Optimize vitamin D** - Target 60-80 ng/mL
- **Assess adrenal function** - Cortisol patterns affect thyroid
- **Evaluate gut health** - 20% of T4 to T3 conversion occurs in the gut
- **Screen for infections** - EBV, Lyme can affect thyroid

### Step 2: Medication Options

1. **Levothyroxine (T4 only)**
   - Starting dose: 25-50 mcg daily
   - Increase by 25 mcg every 6-8 weeks
   - Take on empty stomach, 30-60 min before food

2. **Combination T4/T3 Therapy**
   - Consider if poor T4 to T3 conversion
   - Typical ratio: 4:1 to 10:1 (T4:T3)
   - Start T3 at 5 mcg, increase slowly

3. **Natural Desiccated Thyroid (NDT)**
   - Contains T4, T3, T2, T1, and calcitonin
   - Starting dose: 15-30 mg (0.25-0.5 grain)
   - Increase by 15 mg every 4-6 weeks

### Step 3: Lifestyle Modifications

- **Diet**: Consider gluten-free trial (especially with Hashimoto's)
- **Stress management**: Chronic stress increases reverse T3
- **Sleep**: 7-9 hours, support circadian rhythm
- **Exercise**: Moderate intensity, avoid overtraining

## Follow-Up Schedule

- **Initial adjustment**: Recheck labs every 6-8 weeks
- **Once stable**: Every 3-6 months
- **Annual**: Full thyroid panel + related markers

## Related Conditions to Evaluate

When treating hypothyroidism, always consider:
- **Adrenal fatigue** - Thyroid-adrenal axis dysfunction
- **Iron deficiency** - Required for T4 synthesis and conversion
- **B12 deficiency** - Common with autoimmune thyroid
- **Gut health issues** - Affect absorption and conversion
