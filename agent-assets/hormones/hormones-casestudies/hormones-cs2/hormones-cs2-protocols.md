## Patient Presentation

- **Age:** 27 years old
- **Sex:** Female
- **Chief Complaints:** Fatigue, hair loss

## Diagnostic Findings

### HRV Analysis
- **System Energy Available:** 6-7 (borderline healthy/athlete range)
- **Stress Response:** 3
- **PNS Pattern:** Poor parasympathetic response when stressed, low PNS engagement
- **SNS Pattern:** Elevated sympathetic response, not staying calm in stress zones

### Brainwave Analysis
- **Alpha:** 7% (very low - indicates potential pain, disconnection from Schumann resonance)
- **Beta:** 23% (elevated - indicates midbrain setting point too high)
- **Gamma:** 42% (high - racing brain, excess neural activity)
- **Delta:** 17% (elevated waking delta - low direct current)
- **Theta:** 10%

**Key Finding:** Beta dominant pattern with low alpha indicates central pain issues and circadian rhythm dysfunction.

### D-Pulse Analysis
- **Stress Index:** 32 (low end of normal 10-100)
- **Index of Vegetative Balance:** 64
- **Brain Activity:** 83% (slightly reduced)
- **Thyroid:** 100%
- **Sacrum:** 83% (slightly reduced)
- **Cervical:** 93%

**Key Finding:** Nearly perfect D-Pulse with biotoxic illness can indicate the machine is being "tricked" - common pattern in biotoxic illness.

### Urinalysis
- **pH:** 6.47 (LOW - abnormal, under 6.5 threshold indicates metabolic acidosis)
- **Protein:** 15 (ABNORMAL - indicates MSH issues, UB rate dysfunction, cellular debris)
- **Specific Gravity:** 1.015 (normal)
- **VCS Score:** 31/32 (passing, but borderline)

## Deal Breakers Identified

1. **Low pH (6.47)** - Under 6.5 is problematic, indicates energetic debt
2. **Protein in urine** - Cellular debris, MSH/UB rate dysfunction
3. **Low Alpha (7%)** - Pain indicator, circadian rhythm issues
4. **High Beta/Gamma** - Midbrain set point too high, racing brain
5. **Poor PNS response** - Autonomic nervous system dysfunction
