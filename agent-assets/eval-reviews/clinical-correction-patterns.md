# BFM Clinical Correction Patterns — Derived from Dr. Rob's Review (March 2026)

Source: Dr. Rob's review of 5 patient evaluations (DH, GD, GG, JW, TH), graded and corrected March 2026.
These patterns are the highest-priority training rules for the BFM eval system.

---

## RULE 1: SNS Balance — Only When SNS Is Clearly Switched

**Pattern**: Do NOT trigger SNS Balance unless the SNS is clearly switched (going in the wrong direction under stress).

**Wrong**: Triggering SNS Balance whenever PNS is negative and SNS is positive.
**Right**: SNS Balance is triggered ONLY when the sympathetic nervous system is clearly switched — meaning it moves in the wrong direction during stress testing (ortho/Valsalva).

**Cases where SNS Balance was correctly applied**: DH (SNS clearly switched), TH (SNS clearly switched with fight-or-flight freeze)
**Cases where SNS Balance was incorrectly suggested**: GD (vagus nerve pattern, not SNS switch), GG (vagus pattern, not SNS switch), JW (lower-left depletion = Midbrain Support, not SNS Balance)

**Clinical Rule**: A negative PNS with positive SNS does NOT automatically mean SNS is switched. If the pattern is primarily vagus nerve dysfunction (E/I low, deep breathing barely helps), the correct trigger is Vagus Support, NOT SNS Balance.

---

## RULE 2: Vagus Protocol Order — Support First, Trauma Second

**Pattern**: ALWAYS start with Vagus Support. Only escalate to Vagus Trauma if Vagus Support is insufficient.

**Wrong**: Starting with Vagus Trauma because deep breathing worsens PNS or because vagus dysfunction is severe.
**Right**: Vagus Support calms the midbrain and medulla and attempts to get the vagus nerve firing. Vagus Trauma is the escalation, not the starting point.

**Sequencing**: Vagus Support → (reassess after 2-4 weeks) → Vagus Trauma if insufficient

**Dr. Rob's exact note**: "Vagus Support needs to come first because it will help calm the midbrain and medulla and then try to get the vagus on. If that isn't sufficient, then we use Vagus Trauma on top of that."

## RULE 3: Cell Synergy Dosing — Always 1 Scoop Unless Specific Trigger

**Pattern**: Cell Synergy ALWAYS starts at 1 scoop. Never 2-3 scoops unless there is a specific trigger.

**Triggers for double dose (2 scoops)**:
- "Be More Attentive" finding on HRV
- Alpha/Theta ratio extremely uneven AND not resolving

**Wrong**: Recommending 2-3 scoops for critically low pH or catastrophic organ scores.
**Right**: 1 scoop is the starting dose regardless of severity. Double dose (2 scoops) only for Be More Attentive or persistent Alpha/Theta imbalance.

**Note on Be More Attentive**: "Double dose" means 2 scoops, NOT 4 scoops. Cell Synergy default is 1 scoop, so double = 2.

## RULE 4: Day 1 Supplements — In-Office Testing Only

**Pattern**: Day 1 recommendations should be based ONLY on what is found during in-office testing and deal breakers. Do NOT recommend supplements that require lab confirmation.

**Day 1 core supplements** (from in-office testing):
- Cell Synergy (1 scoop)
- Tri-Salts (if pH < 6.2)
- Pectasol-C (if VCS failed)
- Serculate (if Heart is low on D-Pulse)
- CoQ10 (if Heart is low on D-Pulse)
- X-39 (ALL patients get X-39 on Day 1)

**Week 1-2 supplements** (from lab results):
- Vitamin D (only AFTER 25-OH Vitamin D lab confirms deficiency)
- IP6 Gold (only AFTER Ferritin lab confirms elevation)
- Homocysteine Factor (only AFTER Homocysteine lab confirms elevation)
- Integra Cell (only AFTER CRP lab confirms elevation)
- Adipothin (after leptin assessment)

**Wrong**: Recommending Vitamin D, Integra Cell, or other lab-dependent supplements before labs are obtained.
**Right**: Day 1 = in-office findings only. Lab-dependent supplements wait for lab results.

## RULE 5: X-39 — Triggered by Protein in Urine or Single-Digit D-Pulse Organs

**Pattern**: X-39 (stem cell stimulation patch) is NOT automatic for all patients. It is triggered by specific findings.

**Triggers for X-39 on Day 1**:
- Protein found in the urine
- Most or all D-Pulse organ percentages in single digits (1-9%)

**NOT a trigger**: X-39 is not given to every patient by default. It requires one of the above findings.

**Dosage**: 12 hours on / 12 hours off; rotate placement every 2-3 days. Nightly; C7 or below belly button.

## RULE 6: Leptin Must Be Addressed Before MSH

**Pattern**: VCS failure indicates biotoxins present → biotoxins make the person leptin resistant → leptin resistance leads to low MSH. Must address Leptin FIRST.

**Correct cascade**: VCS Failed → Biotoxins Present → Leptin Resistant → Low MSH
**Correct protocol order**: Biotoxin + Leptin Resist + Adipothin + leptin-style diet → THEN Pit A Support + Pars Intermedia (MSH settings)

**Wrong**: Jumping to MSH support (Pit A, Pars Intermedia) without addressing leptin first.
**Right**: Leptin is the primary hormonal gatekeeper. Must be lowered before other hormones respond.

**Cyto Lower Gate**: MSH must be lab-confirmed > 8 before running Cyto Lower. Duration: 1 hour 45 minutes.

## RULE 7: Five Levers — Group Together in Phase 2

**Pattern**: The "Five Levers" (Pineal Support, Leptin Resist, Pit A Support, Pars Intermedia, and related protocols) should be grouped together, typically in Phase 2.

**Dr. Rob's exact note**: "Keep the 5 Levers in Phase 2 together — Pineal, Leptin, Pit A, and Pars Intermedia."

## RULE 8: Lower-Left Quadrant ANS Pattern = Midbrain Support

**Pattern**: When BOTH SNS and PNS are negative (lower-left quadrant on HRV chart), this is total autonomic depletion — NOT SNS switching.

**Wrong**: Triggering SNS Balance for lower-left quadrant depletion.
**Right**: Trigger Midbrain Support. This pattern indicates total autonomic exhaustion requiring energy restoration, not correction of a switched response.

**Note**: This pattern may not be listed correctly on the clinical flowchart as it is a more atypical finding.

---

## RULE 9: Red Dot on Blue Dot = Locus Coeruleus

**Pattern**: When the red (stressed) dot is superimposed directly over the blue (calm) dot on the HRV chart, this indicates a fight-or-flight "freeze" pattern.

**Frequency**: Locus Coeruleus — should be one of the main settings used alongside other triggered protocols.

## RULE 10: High Baroreceptor Sensitivity = CSF Support

**Pattern**: When the HRV interpretation page indicates "Baroreceptor sensitivity is on a high level," this triggers the CSF Support frequency.

**Note**: This is found on the HRV conclusion/interpretation page, not on the main chart.

**Pattern**: Elevated BUN/Creatinine ratio on lab work triggers the EMF Cord frequency setting.

**Pattern**: Low Specific Gravity (e.g., 1.005 when normal is 1.010-1.020) triggers the need for Deuterium Drops supplement.

## RULE 13: Lab Retest Timing — 3 Months, Not 6 Weeks

**Pattern**: LabCorp bloodwork is typically not re-run until the 3-month mark, NOT 6 weeks.

**In-office retesting** (DePulse, HRV, Ortho, Valsalva, UA, VCS): Can be done at 2-4 week intervals.
**Lab retest**: 3 months from initial labs.

---

## RULE 14: Session Frequency — Be More Attentive Means 3+/Week

**Pattern**: Typical patients are seen 2x/week. When "Be More Attentive" shows up, increase to MORE than 2x/week (ideally 3x/week).

**Wrong**: "Minimum 2x/week for Be More Attentive" — that's the baseline for everyone.
**Right**: Be More Attentive = 3+ sessions/week, especially in the first 4-6 weeks.

---

## RULE 15: No Fake Protocols

**Pattern**: Only recommend frequency protocols that actually exist. Do NOT invent protocol names.

**Protocols that do NOT exist**:
- "Brain Activity Support" — not a real setting
- "Stress Index Regulation" — not a real setting
- "Cervical Support" — not a real setting
- "Immune Support" (as a standalone frequency) — not a real setting

**Rule**: If unsure whether a protocol name is valid, reference it as a clinical goal rather than a specific frequency name.

---

## RULE 16: Arrhythmia Awareness

**Pattern**: Bigeminy and trigeminy on HRV/Valsalva indicate active cardiac arrhythmias. These can inflate Valsalva ratios and adaptability scores, creating paradoxical readings.

**Clinical implications**:
- Monitor arrhythmia pattern at every session
- No aggressive detox until arrhythmias stabilize
- No cold thermogenesis
- Valsalva ratio may appear "athletic" but is artifact of arrhythmia
